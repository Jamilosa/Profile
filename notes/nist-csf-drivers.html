I. Introduction

Laws mandates organizations to adopt structured cybersecurity frameworks to meet legal obligations.

II. Key Points

1. Cybersecurity Enhancement Act of 2014 (CEA)

US federal law that aims to improve cybersecurity through public-private partnership, enhance research and development, and promote workforce education and awareness. Also known as Public Law No: 113-274

2. Executive Order 13636

Titled Improving Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity, issued on Feb 2013 due to increasing cyber threats to critical infrastructure. It focuses on three areas:

  • information sharing
  • privacy
  • adoption of cybersecurity practices

National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) was directed to develop a cybersecurity framework from existing voluntary consensus standards and industry best practices.

3. Executive Order 13800: Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA)

Titled Strengthening the Cybersecurity of Federal Networks and Critical Infrastructure, issued on May 2017.

  • Mandates the framework for all government agencies
  • Still voluntary for everyone else

Requires agencies to:

  • Inventory computer systems
  • Identify and provide appropriate security protections
  • Develop, document, and implement agency-wide information security program
4. Computer Fraud & Abuse Act of 1986 (CFAA)

Intended to reduce cracking of computer systems and to address federal computer-related offenses.

Mainly for financial and government computer systems but later include civilians' computer and even cellphones.

5. Electronic Communications Privacy Act of 1986 (ECPA)

Extended the restrictions on wire taps and other electronic communications in transit
Protects stored electronic communication
Added pen/traps provisions that permits tracking of telephone communications (with court order)

6. Privacy Act of 1974

Now covers personal identifiable information (PII)

  • Restrict disclosure
  • Increase right of access to agency records
  • Grant individuals the right to seek amendment
  • Establish a code of fair information practices
